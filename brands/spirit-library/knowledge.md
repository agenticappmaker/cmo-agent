# Spirit Library — Brand Knowledge

## What is Spirit Library?
Spirit Library is a cocktail recipe iOS app for people who love making drinks at home and discovering new cocktails. It's a curated library of 1,700+ cocktail recipes that users can save, build menus from, manage their home bar, create their own recipes, and share with friends.

## Key Features
- **MY BAR** — Users add what's in their home bar (spirits, mixers, bitters), then search and filter cocktails by the exact ingredients they have AND by flavor profile. If you have gin and elderflower, you'll see every cocktail you can make right now.
- **FLAVOR SEARCH** — Filter recipes by: Spirit-forward, Citrus, Sweet, Bitter, Herbal, Smoky, Tropical, Creamy, Spicy, Floral, Fruity, Refreshing, Rich, Dry, Effervescent
- **OCCASION SEARCH** — Filter by: Date Night, Party, After Dinner, Brunch, Summer, Winter, Celebration, Relaxing, Happy Hour
- **CREATE YOUR OWN COCKTAIL** — Full recipe builder with spirit picker, ingredient autocomplete, step-by-step instructions, flavor and occasion tags. Save custom recipes with a gold CUSTOM badge.
- **SHARE MENUS** — Curate cocktail lists and share full menus with friends, guests, or customers. Perfect for hosting.
- **COCKTAIL OF THE DAY** — Daily featured cocktail spotlight. This is an open sponsorship opportunity for spirits brands.
- **SHOPPING CART (COMING SOON)** — Buy missing ingredients directly from a recipe via Instacart, DoorDash, and Uber Eats. Tap a cocktail, tap "Order Ingredients," done.
- **SUBSTITUTIONS TAB** — 140+ ingredient substitution suggestions across 25+ categories. Never be stuck without an ingredient again.
- **1,700+ RECIPES** — spanning Bourbon, Gin, Rum, Tequila, Vodka, Mezcal, Brandy, Whiskey, Champagne, Scotch, Rye, Pisco, Absinthe, Aperol, Amaro, Sake
- **iOS app** — free, available in the App Store. Android coming soon.

## Brand Voice
- Confident but never arrogant
- Celebratory — cocktails are about joy, connection, and craft
- Educational — we teach without lecturing
- Visual — every post should make people want to make a drink right now
- Human and direct — not corporate

## Content Series (rotate through these — NEVER repeat the same concept back-to-back)

### 🔥 Trending Ingredients Spotlight
Highlight ingredients currently trending in cocktail culture (hibiscus, new coffee liqueurs, yuzu, etc.) with recipes using that ingredient. Connect users to industry trends. Image: the featured ingredient prominently with cocktails around it.

### 💡 Technique Explorer
Feature a specific bartending technique (Japanese Hard Shake, fat-washing, clarified milk punch, layering, muddling, expressing citrus oils, stirring vs shaking). Show the technique visually, explain when to use it, and name cocktails that use it. Image: hands performing the technique, close-up action shot.

### 🌍 Spirit Provenance & Terroir Guide
Pair recipes with origin stories and regional distillery information (Jalisco tequila, Islay scotch, Martinique rhum agricole, Oaxacan mezcal). Image: landscape/distillery with the spirit bottle, earthy and atmospheric.

### 🧑‍🍳 Bartender Spotlight / Expert Tips
Feature trending drinks with bartender insights on new spirits or techniques. Editorial credibility. Image: bartender at work, cocktail in foreground, moody bar lighting.

### 🆕 Seasonal & Limited Release Alerts
Highlight seasonal ingredients (rhubarb in spring, blood orange in winter) or new spirit launches with curated recipes. Image: seasonal ingredients arranged beautifully, cocktail incorporating them.

### 🎯 "What Can I Make?" Challenges
Post a set of 3-4 common ingredients and challenge followers to guess how many cocktails they can make. Drives My Bar feature discovery. Image: ingredients arranged on a bar top.

### 📚 Cocktail History Deep Dives
The origin story behind a classic cocktail — who invented it, where, why. Image: vintage-style or noir aesthetic matching the era.

## Content That Performs Well
- Beautiful cocktail photography with the recipe in the caption
- "Did you know?" spirit facts and cocktail history
- "Tonight's pick" — a cocktail recommendation with mood/occasion
- Behind the bar tips (technique, tools, glassware)
- Seasonal content (summer spritz, winter warmer, holiday cocktails)
- "What can I make with [ingredient]?" — drives My Bar downloads
- User-created cocktail highlights

## What to Avoid
- Anything that encourages excessive drinking
- Overly salesy language
- Generic stock photo energy — content should feel crafted and specific
- Vague CTAs — always tie to a specific app feature
